Eilidh MacNicol

During my MRC-funded PhD at King's College London, my research intended to
characterise the changes in structural and functional brain networks which occur during healthy ageing.

I graduated from the University of Glasgow in 2013 with a BSc(Hons) in neuroscience,
having taken modules in human biology, psychology, and chemistry.
Following a short sabbatical for travel,
I graduated with distinction from King's College London with a MSc in neuroimaging.

My PhD was an opportunity to realise my abilities in data analysis;
I developed pre-processing pipelines for functional and structural images
that make use of Bash, Python, R, and MATLAB environments,
and explored various advanced statistical analysis methods.
I also gained an enthusiasm for open science: using git to share my code,
porting code to open-source languages, and sharing results with pre-prints.
